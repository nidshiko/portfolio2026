Un Año de Pole

Personal zine documenting one year of pole sport — the studio, the body, the gap between expectation and reality. Analog illustration, layout design, and self-published print run.

Un Año de Pole started as a way to process a year of learning something completely new with my body. Pole sport is intense, funny, humbling — and I wanted to document that honestly.

The zine combines observational illustration with a loose editorial structure: the studio painted from memory, a spread of figure studies cataloguing tricks, a humorous expectation/reality comparison, and portraits of the people who share that space.

Everything was drawn analog with color pencil and assembled in InDesign. It was self-published and printed as a physical object — which is the only format that felt right for it.
