El Centauro de Guerin

Art book design for Maurice de Guérin's prose poem, with original illustrated cover and four postcards. Exhibited at Museo de la Cárcova, Buenos Aires.

El Centauro is a prose poem by the French Romantic writer Maurice de Guérin — a mythological meditation on nature, belonging, and the body that is half animal. The brief was to design an edition of the text that felt as elemental as the writing itself.

The book uses a restrained typographic system — generous white space, classical serif, a glossary of mythological references. The cover illustration is a circular composition in color pencil: a sun reflected in dark water, a form that contains both light and void.

The project also produced four illustrated postcards, each a circular world — an eye that holds a landscape inside it, a figure surrounded by flowing fire, a centaur silhouetted at dusk. The complete edition was exhibited at Museo de la Cárcova as part of the Diplomatura en Artes del Libro at UBA.
